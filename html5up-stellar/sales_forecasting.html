Objective

The objective of this project was to develop a reliable forecasting model to predict quarterly sales for a company using historical data from 2019 and 2020. By employing ARIMA (AutoRegressive Integrated Moving Average) and Seasonal Naive forecasting methods, the goal was to capture the inherent seasonality and trends in the sales data to project the sales figures for the first quarter of 2021.

Challenge

The primary challenge was to accurately forecast future sales by understanding and modeling the historical sales data's patterns, trends, and seasonality. The task involved preprocessing the data, checking for stationarity, and selecting appropriate forecasting models that could handle the seasonal fluctuations observed in the sales data, while not being impeded by the limited historical data.

Innovation

This project utilized ARIMA modeling, and then followed up with the Seasonal Naive forecasting approach, due to the difficulty ARIMA was having capturing the seasonality of the data. The Seasonal Naive method leveraged historical seasonal patterns to predict future sales. This approach highlights the importance of flexibility when choosing the right model for your data to produce the most robust forecasts.

Process

The project followed a structured process, including:

  • Data cleaning and preprocessing to prepare the monthly sales data for analysis.
  • Conducting stationarity tests to ensure the data's suitability for time series forecasting.
  • Utilizing pmdarima's auto_arima function to find the optimal parameters for the ARIMA model, then running ARIMA.
  • Pivoting to a Seasonal Naive forecasting method to exploit the data's seasonal patterns.
  • Comparing the forecasts against actual sales data from Q1 2020 to evaluate the models' accuracy.
  • Visualizing the forecasts and their confidence intervals to present the findings clearly.

Technology Stack

  • Pandas: For data manipulation and analysis, essential for preprocessing the sales data.
  • NumPy: Utilized for numerical operations, particularly in implementing the Seasonal Naive method.
  • Matplotlib & Seaborn: For data visualization, enabling the effective presentation of forecasts and historical data trends./li>
  • pmdarima: Employed for its auto_arima function, facilitating the ARIMA model's parameter selection.
  • SQLite3: For database management, demonstrating the integration of SQL in data analytics projects.
  • Python: The core programming language for implementing the forecasting models and data analysis.

Outcomes

While ARIMA helped in understanding the underlying trends and patterns, this model did exhibit a confidence interval I was not comfortable with. Limited data meant that we were unable to use models better suited to seasonal forecasting such as Seasonal AutoRegressive Integrated Moving Average (SARIMA) or Holt-Winters Exponential Smoothing. In the end, pivoting to the Seasonal Naive approach capitalized on the seasonal patterns observed in previous years, to yield a next-quarter forecast with a much more conservative variability range.
